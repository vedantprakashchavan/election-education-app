What is an Election?

An election is a formal democratic process where citizens choose their representatives. In India, all elections are overseen by the Election Commission of India (ECI) — an independent constitutional body established under Article 324.

Types of Elections in India
Lok SabhaNational Parliament — held every 5 years
Rajya SabhaUpper house — elected by state legislators
Vidhan SabhaState assembly — held every 5 years
Local BodyMunicipal & Panchayat elections
Key Constitutional Facts

• Lok Sabha has 543 elected seats
• Rajya Sabha has 245 seats (233 elected + 12 nominated)
• Simple majority = 272 seats to form government
• ECI was established on 25 January 1950
• India has over 900 million registered voters

Election Phases — From Announcement to Government
Phase 1
Announcement of Elections
ECI releases the election schedule. Model Code of Conduct (MCC) comes into effect immediately, restricting the ruling government from announcing new policies.
Phase 2
Nomination Filing
Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. A security deposit must be paid. Candidates need a proposer from their constituency.
Phase 3
Scrutiny & Withdrawal
Nominations are examined for eligibility. Candidates may withdraw until the last date for withdrawal. Final candidate list is published.
Phase 4
Election Campaign
Parties and candidates campaign. All campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling day — this is called the "silence period" or campaign blackout.
Phase 5
Polling Day
Registered voters visit their assigned polling booth and cast their vote using EVMs. Polling hours are generally 7 AM to 6 PM.
Phase 6
Counting & Results
Votes are counted on counting day. The Returning Officer declares the winning candidate. Results are compiled by ECI and published officially.
Phase 7
Government Formation
The party or coalition with a majority forms the government. The President invites them to form the government and the PM/CM is sworn in.
How to Vote — Step by Step Guide
1
Check voter registration
Verify your name on the Electoral Roll at voterportal.eci.gov.in or call the Voter Helpline 1950. If not registered, submit Form 6 online.
2
Get your Voter ID (EPIC)
Obtain your Elector's Photo Identity Card. You can also use Aadhaar, Passport, PAN card, Driving Licence, or MNREGA job card as valid ID.
3
Find your polling booth
Locate your assigned polling station from your Voter Slip (delivered to your address) or via the Voter Helpline App.
4
Visit on polling day
Arrive at your polling station during voting hours (7 AM – 6 PM). Stand in the queue and wait for your turn as directed by polling officials.
5
Verify identity & register
Show your ID card. Officials verify your name in the Electoral Roll, mark your left index finger with indelible ink, and provide a ballot slip.
6
Cast your vote on EVM
Enter the voting compartment. Press the button next to your chosen candidate on the EVM. The VVPAT prints a paper slip visible for 7 seconds to verify your vote.
✓
Your vote is cast!
Leave the booth. Your vote is completely secret and protected by the Representation of the People Act, 1951. You have exercised your democratic right!
Any Indian citizen who is 18 years or older on the qualifying date, and whose name appears on the Electoral Roll of their constituency, is eligible to vote. Non-resident Indians (NRIs) can also register to vote in their home constituency.
NOTA stands for "None of the Above." Introduced in 2013 by the Supreme Court, it allows voters to formally reject all candidates on the ballot without spoiling the vote. Even if NOTA gets the most votes, the candidate with the highest votes among real candidates wins.
The MCC is a set of guidelines issued by ECI to regulate conduct of political parties, candidates, and the ruling government from election announcement until results. It restricts freebies, hate speech, use of government resources for campaigning, and bribery.
You must update your voter registration to your current address. Submit Form 8A online at voterportal.eci.gov.in to transfer your registration to your new constituency. You can only vote where you are registered.
Visit your local Booth Level Officer (BLO) or submit Form 6 online to enroll. Voter registration drives are conducted before every election. You can also call the helpline 1950 for assistance.
EVMs in India are standalone devices with no internet or network connectivity. They are manufactured by ECIL and BEL under strict supervision. VVPAT machines provide paper-based verification. EVMs undergo First Level Checking (FLC) and mock polls before every election.
General candidates pay ₹25,000 for Lok Sabha and ₹10,000 for Vidhan Sabha. SC/ST candidates pay half. The deposit is forfeited if the candidate fails to secure more than 1/6th of total valid votes in their constituency.
A hung parliament occurs when no single party or pre-poll alliance wins a clear majority (272+ seats in Lok Sabha). This leads to coalition government formation, where multiple parties come together to form a majority government.

Election Process Quiz complete
ECI — Election Commission of India
The independent constitutional body under Article 324 responsible for administering all elections in India — national, state, and district level.
EVM — Electronic Voting Machine
A standalone electronic device used to cast and count votes. Consists of a Control Unit (with polling officer) and Balloting Unit (in voting compartment).
VVPAT — Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail
A device attached to the EVM that prints a paper slip showing the candidate voted for, visible for 7 seconds before dropping into a sealed box for audit purposes.
EPIC — Elector's Photo Identity Card
The official voter identity card issued by ECI. Commonly known as Voter ID card. Serves as proof of identity and voter registration.
NOTA — None of the Above
An option on the EVM ballot allowing voters to formally reject all candidates without spoiling their vote. Introduced in 2013 per Supreme Court directive.
MCC — Model Code of Conduct
Guidelines issued by ECI governing behavior of political parties, candidates, and the government from election announcement until result declaration.
Constituency
A defined geographic area whose registered voters collectively elect one representative to a legislative body (Parliament or State Assembly).
Electoral Roll
The official register of all eligible voters in a constituency. Also called the voter list. Maintained and updated by ECI through Booth Level Officers.
Returning Officer
The designated government official responsible for conducting the election in a constituency and officially declaring the winning candidate.
BLO — Booth Level Officer
A government official assigned to each polling booth to maintain the Electoral Roll, assist with voter registration, and resolve voter grievances.
Indelible Ink
A special ink applied to a voter's left index finger after casting their vote. It cannot be washed off for several days, preventing double voting.
Hung Parliament / Assembly
A situation where no single party wins an outright majority of seats, requiring coalition formation to establish a government.
Whip
An official directive issued by a political party to its legislators instructing them how to vote on a particular issue in the legislature.
Anti-Defection Law
Under the Tenth Schedule of the Constitution, a legislator can be disqualified if they vote against their party's direction or voluntarily give up party membership.
